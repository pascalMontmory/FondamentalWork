# Short-list of open conjectures suitable for formal attacks

Date: 2026-06-10.

The aim is not to list famous impossible targets, but to isolate conjectures
where a concise structural lemma can move the problem.

## A. Casas-Alvero conjecture

Statement. If a degree \(d\) complex polynomial \(f\) has a nonconstant common
factor with each derivative \(f^{(1)},\ldots,f^{(d-1)}\), then
\[
  f(x)=c(x-a)^d.
\]

Why attractive:

* algebraic and formal;
* derivative equations give exact constraints;
* many degrees are solved, but the general problem remains delicate;
* the smallest open degree is reported as \(20\) in standard summaries.

Risk:

* recent preprints claim broad solutions, so every claim must be checked
against the literature.

## B. Legendre's conjecture

Statement. For every \(n\ge1\), there is a prime in
\[
  (n^2,(n+1)^2).
\]

Why attractive:

* elementary statement;
* exact reformulation as a residue-covering problem;
* Gaussian-integer substitutions \(n^2+t^2\) create a non-standard route.

Risk:

* proving the full conjecture would imply a major prime-gap breakthrough.

## C. Erdős-Ulam rational distance problem

Statement. Does the plane contain a dense set whose pairwise distances are
rational?

Why attractive:

* geometric and algebraic;
* conditional negative results exist via Bombieri-Lang or abc;
* one can prove unconditional restrictions on algebraic curves.

Risk:

* the full problem is tied to deep arithmetic geometry.

## D. Seymour second-neighborhood conjecture

Statement. Every oriented graph has a vertex whose second out-neighborhood is
at least as large as its first out-neighborhood.

Why attractive:

* clean extremal formulation;
* many special classes are known;
* local structural lemmas can matter.

Risk:

* full proof is graph-theoretically deep; finite cases tend to become SAT-like.

## Ranking for a concise proof attempt

1. Casas-Alvero: best match for formal equations.
2. Legendre via Gaussian integers: best non-standard idea, high risk.
3. Seymour: useful but likely computational for new finite cases.
4. Erdős-Ulam: elegant but too deep for immediate closure.
